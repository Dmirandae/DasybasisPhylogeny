While classical morphology provides a foundation for insect classification, homoplasy can lead to misinterpretations (Rannala et al., 2015). To address this, researchers are increasingly integrating molecular techniques to complement morphological data and distinguish between homoplastic and homologous traits (Foster & Hughes, 2015).

Overall, the relationship between homoplasy and classical morphology is pivotal in entomology. By recognizing the impact of homoplasy and combining different analytical approaches, scientists can refine classifications and gain a more accurate understanding of insect diversity and evolution (Misof et al., 2014).
